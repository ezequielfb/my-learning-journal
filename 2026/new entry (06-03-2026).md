# Learning Log: System Modeling & Requirements Engineering

## Case Study: Pousadas Férias no Norte
**Date:** March 06, 2026  
**Focus:** UML, Requirements Engineering, Business Rules.

### Why I am adding this to my Journal
Even though my primary career focus is **Artificial Intelligence** and **Python**, this UML and Java/Hibernate case study is a fundamental part of my Software Engineering degree. 

I believe that:
1. **Solid Foundations:** Professional AI solutions require robust underlying systems. Understanding Entities, Relationships, and Business Rules is the first step to building quality data pipelines.
2. **Process Mapping:** Before automating a business with AI, I must be able to map its manual and legacy processes (like the telephone booking workflow in this case).
3. **Bridge to AI:** While this project uses traditional MVC/Java, I am already documenting how to evolve it using **Regression Models** for Dynamic Pricing and **NLP** for automated booking confirmation.

### Key Engineering Tasks:
* **Requirement Elicitation:** Translated a business description into 13 Functional Requirements (FR) and 10 Business Rules (BR).
* **System Constraints:** Documented Non-Functional Requirements (NFR) focused on Reliability and Cloud Infrastructure.
* **Architectural Vision:** Structured the logic for Check-in/Check-out cycles and consumption tracking.
